Cisco ACI Advanced Deployment Workshop

Offering Formats

This course is offered in the following formats:

  • Live, Instructor-Led Training (schedule)

Course Overview

In this 5-day Cisco ACI Deployment Workshop course, you will become an expert at the architecture and implementation of Cisco® Application Centric Infrastructure (ACI), including the planning, design, and implementation. This includes critical information on how to listen for and align with your business' key, strategic initiatives and add enormous value, driving lines of business and providing FastIT or ITaaS, as part of a larger cloud architecture

It is designed for Architects, Systems Engineers and Field Engineers who are architecting, implementing and managing Cisco ACI. The course reviews the key concepts behind ACI’s application-centric infrastructure and policy-based view of the solution.  You will then spend 3 days configuring over and again every aspect of the ACI solution, including configuring tenants, application network profiles (ANP), contracts, endpoints, VMM integration and how to connect the ACI Fabric to an external L2 and L3 networks, along with L4-L7 services.  Migration strategies for existing applications to an ACI fabric will be discussed and automation of APIC REST API through scripting (JSON/XML).  The course will include intense hands-on labs on real physical hardware of all of the key functions of ACI.

Upon completion of the course, students will be familiar with:

  • Pre-Deployment Interview/Information gathering process
  • Out of the box setup and configuration of an ACI fabric
  • Scenario driven configuration principals for:
    • Tenant Configurations
    • Application Network Profiles (ANP)
    • Contracts, endpoints
    • Virtual Machine Manager (VMM) integration
    • Connecting the ACI fabric to external L2 and L3 networks
    • Connecting fabric to L4 – L7 services (ASA, F5, etc….)

Course Specifics

  • This is a heavily lab-driven course (70:30 ratio lab:lecture), intended to engage and challenge the student into performing the most common but complex customer scenarios when migrating to Cisco Application Centric Infrastructure (ACI) Fabric
  • Hands on lab environments. Each student will have:
    • Dedicated ACI tenant with 4 ESXi hosts and 9 VM’s
    • Dedicated vCenter Appliance
    • Dedicated L2/L3 outside connections
  • Class size will be restricted to 16 students to facilitate more hands on lab time.
  • Each student will have a dedicated Tenant and dedicated ports both on the ACI Leafs and on the external Nexus 5K/6Ks, dedicated 4 ESXi servers, vCenter server and VMs.
  • Students do not share or “partner-up” to perform tasks. Each student independently performs all tasks.

Who Should Participate?

  • System Integrators
  • Network Administrators
  • System Engineers

    • Course Agenda

    Day 1
    Lecture 01 ACI Messaging and Physical Fabric
    Review Reason why ACI including Legacy Deployment models and Why ACI Works. Review Policy Model / Managed Object Model (MIT). Review Fabric->Fabric Policies, Fabric->Access Policies including Switch Profiles, Interface Policies/Policy Groups, Interface Profiles, Domains, Pools and AEPs.
    Lecture 02 Logical Tenants and VxLAN Overlay
    Review Tenants (RBAC), ANPs, EPGs, BDs, PNs/Ctxts, L2 Out, L3 Out and how Common tenant fits into the design picture. Spend good time on L3 options (including DCI).
    Lecture 03 Mgmt Services and Complete Application Picture
    Review Management Tenant with Static node assignment, NTP, DNS, Contracts and how to collect data and Model/Build a complete Application for customer.
    LUNCH BREAK
    Demo 01 Blank Fabric to Management Ready
    Instructor will demonstrate discovering fabric, setup vPC Domain, MP-BGP, Static Node Management Address Assignment, NTP, and DNS.
    Demo 02 Fabric Access Policy
    Instructor will demonstrate complete setup of Fabric Access Policies including Switch Profiles, Interface Policies/Policy Groups, Interface Profiles, Domains, Pools and AEPs for two (2) Tenants.
    Demo 03 Logical Tenant Overlay
    Instructor will demonstrate Setup of a Tenant with BD/VRFs and having routes leaded into the Common Tenant and using DHCP/DNS/LDAP services from the Common Tenant. Instructor will create ANPs, EPGs, BDs, PNs/Ctxts, L2 Out, L3 Out (as well as other design models), including all new 1.1 features if out.
    Wrap-Up/Q&A/Prep for Tomorrow Questions, Doubts, Concerns, Thoughts, Prep for Week
    Instructor will spend time answering questions and preparing students for the week ahead that they will have going from being able to “kind-of” setup ACI, to being 100% completely confident in their abilities to go onsite with a customer and facilitate a successful CPOC that converts to a sale.
    Day 2
