Designing and Deploying VXLAN with EVPN Control Plane on Cisco Nexus 9000

Offering Formats

This course is offered in the following formats:

  • Live, Instructor-Led Training (schedule)
  • Self-Paced, Online Training (coming soon!)

Course Overview

In this 2-day course, you will become experienced with the fundamentals concepts, architecture, design, implementation and maintanence of a VXLAN fabric using MP-BGP SAFI-70 (EVPN) as the control plane, running on CiscoÂ® Nexus 9000 in NX-OS standalone mode.

It is designed for customers, systems engineers, and field engineers who wanting to learn how to. Lumos Nexus 9K VXLAN course include hands-on labs with physical Nexus 9000 hardware, not simulators, which allows the student to validate all of their work in the data plane.

Who Should Attend?

  • Solution Architects
  • System Integrators
  • Network Administrators
  • System Engineers

Recommended Prerequisites

  • Fundamental understanding of L2 and L3 networking
  • Basic understanding of Nexus switches
  • Basic understanding of MP-BGP (helpful but not necessary)

Course Agenda

Day 1
Lecture 01 Introduction to N9K Open NX-OS and VXLAN
Students will become familiar with the desired qualities and attributes of overlays in the DC today, the state of VXLAN technology and its planned evolution and the alignment to Ciscoâs DC Fabric Portfolio and messaging and positioning and how Ciscoâs move to a completely Open NX-OS.
Lab 10 Fabric Underlay IS-IS
Students will begin building the basic L3 underlay to provide ECMP in the fabric.
Lab 02 Basic BGP with Route Reflector
Students will build the basic BGP infrastructure that will later form the VXLAN control plane, using security to ensure VXLAN control plane traffic is not interfered.
Lecture 02 VXLAN Routing and Control Plane
Student will take a more in-depth look at the VXLAN protocol, Symmetric vs Asymmetric IRB, and how MP-BGP SAFI-70 (EVPN) provides an optimum control plane, as well as how to configure all of these on Nexus 9000 switches.
LUNCH BREAK
Lab 03 Multi-Protocol BGP with EVPN
Students will finish building all of the complete MP-BGP EVPN control plane, and ensure that it is forwarding before adding VXLAN L2 MAC and L3 IPs to it
Lab 04 L2 & L3 VXLAN VTEP with EVPN Control Plane
Students will spend the majority of the day in this larger lab configuring all aspects of VXLAN, including L2 & L3 VLAN-to-VNI mapping, VRFs, Anycast SVIs, NVEs, and BGP and EVPN integration. Students will then test and troubleshoot their configurations. Once they have everything working, they will spend some time exploring various show and debug commands, as well as vMotioning VMs from one leaf to another and explore how that impacts control plane learning.
Lab 05 DHCP Relay and VXLAN Routing
Students will spend the remainder of the day working with DHCP Relay, learning about Option 82 and how N9K treats VRFs, as well as routing between VXLAN VNIs using L3 SVIs/VNIs, and explore how this impacts BGP tables.
Day 2
Lab 06 Exploring VXLAN and vMotion BGP Updates with Wireshark
Students will begin by taking an in-depth look at how to setup SPAN and decode Fake ERSPAN Frames using Wireshark. They will vMotion a VM to another Leaf, and sniff traffic to see how it affects BGP Updates, while also taking a look at the VXLAN header and differentiating between L2 forwarding and L3 forwarding.
Lab 07 VXLAN EVPN Border Leaf to External L3 via OSPF
Students will incorporate external prefixes into their VXLAN fabric via OSPF and learn how to control /32 prefixes outside their fabric (whether desired or not).
LUNCH BREAK
Lab 08 VXLAN EVPN Border Leaf to External L3 via BGP
Students will incorporate additional external prefixes into their VXLAN fabric via BGP and learn how to control /32 prefixes outside their fabric (whether desired or not), as well as how to avoid routing loops.
Lab 09 vPC and Anycast VTEPs
Students will incorporate vPC into their VXLAN design and explore how Anycast is used to advertise host MAC and IP addresses as well as externally learnt prefixes via OSPF or BGP.
Lecture 03 VXLAN Designs and DCI
Students will learn how to migrate existing DC designs over to Cisco 9K VXLAN with EVPN, how to scale VXLAN designs, as well as how to position VXLAN/EVPN as a DCI solution vs technologies such as OTV or VPLS.
Lecture 04 VXLAN and EVPN Automation
Students will learn about various ways to automate both the deployment and ongoing management of VXLAN/EVPN networks, including Python, JSON-RPC and/or REST APIs via the Model Driven Programability (MDP)
Lab 01 VTS 2.0 and NFM Integration
If time allows, students will explore the basics of both the VTS 2.0 and NFM systems. Here we will compare and contrast the benefits of Virtual Topology System (VTS) as well as Nexus Fabric Manager (NFM) and their control and programmability of VXLAN/EVPN.
